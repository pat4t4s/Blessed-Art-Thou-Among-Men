CHARACTERS


"No matter the darkness, my faith will guide me—and I will bring you back, Maria."



Joseph


Joseph is the main character in Blessed Art Thou Among Men, the character the player primarily controls throughout the game, and the poster character of the game, alongside his wife Maria. A devoted and religious husband, Joseph faces visions of sin, tests of faith, and personal temptations as he strives to save Maria and stay true to his beliefs.



STORY



The candle flickered, throwing shadows across the walls of the tiny, sickroom where Maria lay. Her shallow breaths were ragged, each one a fragile thread holding her life together. Joseph knelt beside her, clutching her hand, his knuckles white.

“They called me the son of the devil,” he whispered, voice trembling. “A wretch… a sinner… yet here I am, only trying to keep you alive.”

Outside, the wind howled through the twisted trees of the remote village, carrying with it the faint, unnatural whispers of the damned. Joseph rose, torches in the village square illuminating paths that seemed both familiar and grotesquely distorted. Every shadow seemed to leer, every corner whispered his name. He felt it—the pull of something darker than sin, a presence watching, waiting to strike at the cracks in his faith.

Joseph clutched his rosary, murmuring prayers under his breath. He had learned quickly: one missed prayer, one faltering word of faith, and the visions would take hold. Faces of the people he had wronged, acts of theft, violence, and desperation—all twisted into monstrous shapes that reached for him. The deeper he went, the more vivid they became.

Yet he could not stop. Maria’s face, pale but serene, burned in his mind. She was his anchor, his reminder that his sins, terrible as they were, had a purpose. Every vial of medicine, every cursed path through the village, every hallucinatory trap demanded his devotion.

A shadow slithered across the path, whispering of temptation: Abandon her. Take your ease. Give up your faith… Joseph’s fingers clenched the rosary tighter. “Lord, give me strength,” he prayed, voice firm despite the fear. The whisper recoiled, hissed, then vanished.

Hours, maybe days, passed in this dance of light and dark. Joseph’s body ached, his mind teetered on the edge of madness, but he pressed on. Every prayer was a shield, every step a test. The true devil—whatever it was—waited at the end of this trial, but Joseph could not falter. He would not falter.

When he finally returned, medicine in hand, Maria stirred, her eyes fluttering open. Relief washed over him, but even as he held her close, he knew: the real battle was never over. Faith was a living thing, fragile and demanding, and the shadows of sin would follow him wherever he went.

Yet for now, Joseph whispered a final prayer, holding Maria’s hand to his chest: Blessed art thou among men, Lord… keep us safe, and give me strength for what comes next.




"Stay faithful, Joseph… I believe in you, and in us."



Maria


Maria is a central character in Blessed Art Thou Among Men, serving as both the motivation for Joseph’s journey and one of the poster characters of the game. She is Joseph’s devoted and loving wife, whose illness sets the story in motion. Gentle, kind, and resilient, Maria embodies hope and faith, inspiring Joseph to confront visions of sin, tests of belief, and personal temptations in his quest to save her.



STORY



Maria lay in the small, dimly lit room, the weight of illness pressing her into the mattress. The world beyond the window was a dark swirl of wind and shadow, a village she had never left but now seemed strange and frightening. Yet she did not cry. She never cried.

Her hand reached out, almost instinctively, for Joseph’s when he knelt beside her. His presence was both comforting and painful; she saw the fear in his eyes, the guilt in his hands, and the heavy burden he carried. She wanted to tell him not to blame himself, but words failed her. All she could offer was faith, faith that had always been stronger than despair.

Through the nights, she whispered small prayers, hoping they would reach Joseph even if he wandered into the darkness of the village, lost among shadows and visions. Protect him. Give him strength. Bring him back to me. Each breath was a prayer, each faint smile a beacon. She clung to hope like a lifeline, the same hope that had carried them both through better days.

But Maria also felt the creeping presence of something unnatural. Sometimes, the walls of the room seemed to shiver, the air thick with whispers. She saw shapes in the corners of her vision, fleeting and cruel, as though the world itself sought to take her from Joseph. Still, she did not scream. She could not. Her faith, quiet but unwavering, was her shield.

When Joseph finally returned, weary and worn, carrying the medicine she needed, her eyes shone, not with relief alone, but with recognition of his suffering. She reached for him, feeling the weight of his trials, the echoes of his sins, the danger he had faced.

“Joseph…” she whispered, voice fragile but sure. “You kept your faith… and you came back.”

In that moment, the shadows seemed to recoil, if only slightly, and the room, heavy with fear, felt lighter. Maria’s strength was never loud, but it was real. It was the light that guided Joseph, the hope that anchored him, the reason he could face the darkness again.

For Maria, faith was not about grand miracles. It was about enduring love, unwavering hope, and the courage to wait, even when the world itself seemed to conspire against them.
